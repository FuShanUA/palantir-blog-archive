< 02 > Model Training

Model training guidelines shouldbe architecture-enforced.

Governments and leading enterprises are aggressively locking down their data perimeters as AI adoption accelerates. The proliferation of third-party model training poses an existential IP risk: proprietary business knowledge, processes, and competitive differentiation are being silently absorbed into externally-owned model weights. Moreover, these are weights the enterprise does not own, control, or audit.

This intelligent engine exists to restore that power to the enterprise through a series of model training guidelines that are not only policy-based, but also architecture-enforced.

< 1 >

Explicit Data Authorization

Training only occurs on data explicitly authorized by the institution. There is no ambient collection, implicit consent, or scope creep.

< 2 >

Data Sovereignty

Sensitive enterprise data never leaves secure organizational boundaries during any phase of training, fine-tuning, or evaluation. All computation occurs within the infrastructure controlled or explicitly designated by the institution.

< 3 >

Model Ownership

Institutions fully own and control institution-specific model weights, adapters, and fine-tuned artifacts created from their data.

< 4 >

Secure Perimeter Enforcement

Trained models, weights, and derived artifacts remain within an institution’s secure infrastructure at all times. There is no export, replication, or external access without an institution’s explicit, documented authorization.

< 5 >

No Cross-Customer Contamination

Data is never commingled across institutions. Training environments are fully isolated per institutions and enforced architecturally, rather than being dependent on policy adherence.

< 6 >

Data Portability

Institutions can export their data, training configurations, and model artifacts at any time in open, non-proprietary formats. There is no lock-in.

< 7 >

No Competitive Replication

Outputs and information derived from an institution’s data will not be used to build or train products that reproduce or disclose an institution’s confidential information or differentiated business advantage.

< 8 >

Right to Erasure

Upon request, all copies of an institution’s data and customer-specific artifacts can be permanently deleted or access-disabled.

< 9 >

Full Auditability

Every training run is logged with complete provenance, including what data was used, when, by whom, under what configuration, and what model it produced.

< 10 >

Foundation Model Firewall

An institution’s data is never used to improve general-purpose or commercial foundation models. Any exception requires an institution’s explicit, written, and independently revocable consent; and those exceptions are granted per instance, not in perpetuity.
