Impact Study

Optimizing the Promotion Process to Reduce Out-of-Stock and Overstock Waste

At one of Europe’s largest retail groups, sub-optimal product selection and allocation were leading to both out-of-stock and overstock issues — especially around their promotions. The result was eroding margins and loss of revenue.

Previously, the business had relied on a costly manual process to make sense of disconnected systems, reports, and forecasts. Improving this process presented a complex data challenge: it is difficult to make granular store-level purchasing, allocation, and promotion decisions while fully considering changing consumer needs, competitor offerings, supply chain disruptions, and seasonality. The group sought a solution that could automate manual processes, provide recommendations that optimize across a range of factors, and better anticipate evolving consumer behavior.

Solution

A large European retailer has used Foundry to optimize its product promotions and reduce out-of-stock and waste. The platform integrates all relevant information (sales, article, promotions, store and warehouse data) and external data (weather, competitor promotion SKUs and prices) to virtualize the entire supply chain.

Low-code tooling makes it easy for business users to define rules to predict optimal product quantities for promotion. Foundry's 360° modeling helps to operationalize, manage, and monitor forecasting models, which can be incorporated into new decision making workflows to create, manage, and track campaigns. The platform empowers all teams — from purchasing to logistics, to marketing and sales — to select the right goods for promotion, at the right time, and at the right quantity on store level.

Impact

↳ The platform creates a digital twin of the supply chain to power decision making.

↳ The platform powers advanced modeling to recommend optimal stock quantities to allocate at a per store level.

↳ The platform enables collaborative operational workflows to dynamically define and execute promotions.

Within three months, Foundry helped reduce out-of-stock goods by more than 20% and the retail group also reported a reduction in incidences of overstock and waste by more than 25%.

Impact Study

Optimizing the Returns Process to Reduce Waste and Negotiate More Favorable Contracts

One of the world’s largest retailers wanted to optimize its returns operations, which processed tens of millions of products every year. The business wanted a solution to deliver greater transparency into the drivers of high-cost returns. Furthermore, it wanted to power smarter decisions on the front lines to translate insights into better contractual terms, reduced waste, and new efficiency gains.

Previously, it had managed millions of returns through a patchwork of databases and manual processes. The terms of these returns, governed by thousands of distinct contracts, were stored in unstructured formats and isolated from operational data. As a result, stakeholders across the returns process (merchants, store personnel, and returns center operators) lacked visibility into the terms and whether they were adhered to, leading to higher costs, more operational friction, and reduced operating efficiency.

Solution

Foundry digitized the organization’s reverse supply chain by quantifying unstructured contracts across thousands of suppliers into a single pane of glass for smarter decision making. Within weeks, the organization identified keys areas of anomalies and waste, and surfaced valuable optimization opportunities.

Foundry linked all returns-related data and encodes a cost/revenue model at every stage of the process. The model analyzed contracts, sales data, customer service data, and return center data — and delivered outputs through intuitive business applications to users such as merchants, return center personnel, store personnel, and suppliers. Foundry generated alerts on returns anomalies (e.g., returns exceeding an expected threshold), enabling proactive communication with suppliers about emerging issues and mitigate unexpected costs. Foundry empowered negotiations with retailer access to granular real-time returns metrics, including returns performance, returns processing shipping and handling costs, and historical quality issues.

Impact

↳ With instant access to operational business data, the retailer was able to answer supplier questions in near real time.

↳ Foundry's data integration and machine learning capabilities were leveraged to combine and analyze thousands of vendor contracts and amendments.

↳ Armed with product-level business data, the retailer negotiated contracts that more accurately accounted for product performance, reduced friction with suppliers, and lowered costs.

↳ The platform's optimization engine helped the retailer surface opportunities at each stage of the returns cycle to accelerate processing of items in stores and reduced disposal costs of returned items.
