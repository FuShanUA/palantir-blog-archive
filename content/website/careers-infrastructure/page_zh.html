The Production Infrastructure Team

At Palantir, we build software that empowers organizations to seamlessly integrate their data, decisions, and operations.

Many of the world’s most important institutions rely on us so they can make good decisions, quickly. That means we work for the common good — within our organization and with other organizations around the world. Our software has been used to help stop terrorist attacks, fight child exploitation, aid in the discovery of new medical treatments, and more.

We’re maintaining hundreds of customer environments, each consisting of over 150 services. As part of the Production Infrastructure Engineering team, you will help build and operate the infrastructure that makes it possible to provide Palantir’s data platforms as a Platform as a Service (PaaS) within those environments. The tools you design will make it easier to create, deploy, and iterate on secure and highly available software.

In essence, your skills will build a foundation that makes our entire product line operate efficiently, smoothly, and securely.

Enabling Palantir’s business to scale and work with some of the world’s most important institutions, securely.

Rory

Why did you join Palantir?

I knew when I was looking for a new job I was looking for a less conventional frontend developer role than what I had previously done. I wanted to develop in closer collaboration with the product designers and be more involved in the product building process.

What do you love about working here?

There are a lot of kind, intelligent people who are really interested in helping you develop your skills (even if those skills seem outside of your job role!). I’ve always been so floored with how patient people are when they are teaching me new things and letting me experiment with new responsibilities. Also the dogs… I just really love office dogs.

What are you excited about?

I’m really excited to see how we can change and push software management processes. In my experience, these processes can be quite painful, and I’m excited to make these workflows feel less obtuse and, dare I say, enjoyable?

What makes Palantir stand out?

There’s a lot of trust and autonomy granted to you when you come on board. Palantir’s structure has given me the space to be part of the decision making process around what we should build and how we should build it. I find that aspect of working here very empowering.

Charissa (she/her)

Why did you join Palantir?

I first joined Palantir as a Forward Deployed Software Engineer intern in 2018. During every step of the interview process, from the first recruiter call to the last hiring manager interview, I was impressed by every person who interviewed me. The questions they asked — both the behavioral and the technical — were so different from other tech companies, and everyone seemed genuinely interested in hearing my story. At the end of the interview day, I left with the impression that my future coworkers would be thoughtful and would challenge me to grow.

I decided to return full time as a Software Engineer after I saw how impactful my work at Palantir could be, even as an intern. My co-intern and I spent our hack week implementing a new feature to improve documentation for our team — we came up with the idea, coded up the V0, and got the idea adopted into the core product by the end of our internship. After my internship ended, I saw one of my colleagues presenting her philanthropic work at a major tech conference. The combination of caring about the real-world outcomes we enable and believing that I can make an outsized contribution to that impact is what has kept me at Palantir.

What do you love about working here?

The opportunities for mentorship at Palantir, both as a mentor and a mentee, have been extremely rewarding. Access to strong mentorship has empowered me to own technical problems that materially impact the business, even this early in my career. I’ve had the opportunity to pay it forward through mentoring new hires and an intern — playing a role in helping someone else grow in technical ability and confidence keeps me energized at work.

I’ve also found a wonderful community of friends — through projects, identity-based affinity groups (Women-in-Tech and PalAPI), and my starting cohort — who support me through any long-hour days and challenging work.

What are you excited about?

Most recently, I’ve been writing software that enables us to run our platforms, Foundry and Gotham, in air-gapped containerized environments. I’ve loved engaging in open-source container standards as part of this project, and diving into a problem space full of operational challenges that can be improved with Apollo.

What makes Palantir stand out?

If you’re looking for high levels of ownership, access to interesting technical problems, mentorship, and exponential growth, then Palantir may be the right choice for you. If you’re also looking for a more mature company with significant product growth potential, excellent employee benefits (read: top-tier health insurance, generous parental leave, and mental health benefits), and thoughtful colleagues, then Palantir is definitely the right company for you.

Our Values

  • The Best Idea Wins

    No matter what team you’re on, Palantirians approach every problem with an engineering mindset. We speak up when things aren’t right, evaluate ideas on their merits, and fix what’s broken. All voices are equal — we hire people to have an opinion and solve problems, regardless of their role or seniority.

  • Own The Outcome

    We are in the business of delivering outcomes, and empower our employees to bring those outcomes to life. Whether you’re on a dev team, a business team working with customers, or an internal team helping other Palantirians, success isn’t a matter of following procedures or checking boxes — success is delivering something that works.

  • Focus on the Mission

    We view software as a means of effecting change in the world, not as an end unto itself. Our mission is to empower our users, who are grappling with hard problems across public health, manufacturing, defense, and disaster relief. We do this by building software that enables those people to more quickly, easily, and accurately harness data to do their jobs.

Why PI?

Operators driving through the open desert in enemy territory. A hurricane tearing through a city. A global pandemic.

Community leaders, first responders, and national security agencies depend on Palantir to communicate mission-critical information in these rapidly developing situations. No matter where they are.

And we are the foundation that makes it all possible.

Our engineering teams are at the forefront of the infrastructure space — we helped design and build support for Spark-on-Kubernetes and applied a novel approach to ephemeral infrastructure in Kubernetes. We take platforms made up of hundreds of individual services and development teams, and enable them to update across heterogeneous deployment targets, unlocking fleet-wide autonomous management. That’s because we need to make sure our front-facing engineers — and their clients — can use our services wherever they’re needed: from the deepest depths of the ocean to the farthest reaches of outer space.

The Production Infrastructure team is Palantir’s center of innovation. We tackle challenging work in uncharted territory. From enabling rapid continuous deployment for our software to bridging communication between rapidly varying cloud and customer environments, we’re solving complex technical problems full of operational challenges and real-world implications. And that’s what we love about it.
