Beyond Linear Analytics

Most data and analytics tools excel at processing information linearly: from data ingestion to visualization.

However, real-world operations are anything but linear — with operators making distributed decisions simultaneously across the organization.

Connecting analytics to operations requires a different architecture.

Your business is a closed-loop system; operations inform decisions, and decisions inform operations. So why doesn't your software work the same way?

The Platform

Each layer of Foundry works to close the loop between analytics and operations, empowering enterprise-wide decision optimization.

  • Data Integration
  • Model Integration
  • Ontology
  • Workflows
  • Decision Orchestration

Data Integration

Secure, flexible and software-defined data integration — in hours, not months.

We've codified 15+ years of data integration experience into Foundry to operationalize data at scale and pace.

Utilizing 200+ data connectors and an extensible plugin-based paradigm, Foundry can seamlessly synchronize multimodal data from across the enterprise: structured, unstructured, streaming, IoT, transactional, geospatial data, and more.

With Foundry, data integration isn't a periodic extraction and injection; it's a real-time, bidirectional connection: user integrations, transformations, and decisions are captured and fed back into originating systems with full provenance and lineage preserved.

Built-in Role-, Classification-, and Purpose-based access controls enable organizations to institute granular privacy and security controls.

Model Integration

Flexible integration (or registration) of models and business logic.

Bring your own models, or build natively.

Using Foundry's open, interoperable architecture, users can integrate existing, third-party models into the Foundry ontology to operationalize against pertinent problems.

A core principle of Foundry is enabling both data scientists and non-technical business users to collaboratively utilize data and models to make better decisions.

Less technical users are empowered by ML toolkits, no-code model development applications, and out-of-the-box AI services that complement and accelerate Foundry’s full suite of end-to-end AI modeling tools for data scientists.

Ontology

Beyond a data model, the Ontology is an operating layer for the organization.

An enterprise-wide digital twin of both data and models.

By connecting underlying data and models to their real-world business objects - such as Plants, Distribution Centers, or Equipment - the Ontology allows analytics teams, data scientists and business decision-makers to collaborate on a dynamic knowledge asset in real time.

This common lexicon of business Objects enables users to intuitively interact with a digital representation of the enterprise — building custom workflows and team-specific applications with ease.

Workflows

Self-serve analytics, operational application building, and workflows.

By leveraging a common set of objects, actions and relationships, users can develop custom workflows or populate out-of-the-box applications at accelerated timescales.

Because these workflows are composed of the same foundational business objects and actions, the shared Ontology compounds and evolves over time — becoming more robust as more workflows are built and contribute operational knowledge.

Taken together, these facets can drive the marginal cost of application development to zero, over time.

Decision Orchestration

Explore scenarios and sync decisions back to source.

Whether produced through operations, simulations, or AI, decisions can be consistently managed and optimized across business units.

Via a suite of native connectors, Foundry enables bi-directional connection to operational and transactional systems (e.g. ERP, CRM, MES, Asset Config, Edge), allowing decisions made in Foundry to propagate holistically throughout the data landscape.

This extensible orchestration paradigm enables Foundry to serve as connective tissue, linking historically siloed, disconnected systems to power smarter, more informed operations.

Foundry in Action

  • Palantir + NHS
  • Palantir + World Food Programme
  • Palantir + Doosan

The COVID-19 Vaccination programme is the largest vaccination programme ever conducted by NHS. The supply chain is incredibly complex. Some vaccines require ultra-cold storage and can only be moved a limited number of times. The NHS had to equip thousands of vaccination sites across three delivery models, and thousands of NHS staff needed to order vaccines and supplies, communicate readiness, and monitor their vaccination events.

Solution

The NHS quickly configured Foundry with custom applications and new integrations to:

  • Ensure site-readiness across England, including data submission, reporting, and the allocation of supplies

  • Manage the order of vaccines and consumables across all three delivery models, regardless of their ordering process

  • Enable the Royal Logistics Corps and the NHS to accurately prioritise and make vaccine delivery decisions. With Foundry’s automated integrations with logistics partners, The RLC and the NHS facilitated the delivery of vaccine and pre- requisite equipment from source to destination

  • Deliver secure, automated reporting to every stakeholder: from GPs looking to understand performance in their Primary Care Network, to Ministers observing aggregated performance for the entire country
