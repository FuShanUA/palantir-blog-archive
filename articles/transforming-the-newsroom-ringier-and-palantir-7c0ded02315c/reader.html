Transforming the Newsroom: Ringier and Palantir

2021-06-11 · Original →

In this post, Chief Product Officer of Blick-Group and former Editor-in-Chief of Blick.ch, Katia Murmann, and Palantir Deployment Strategist Romain de Ganay discuss the foundations of Ringier AG’s partnership with Palantir and how they’re leveraging the platform to better inform readers.

For more information, watch Palantir and Ringier’s recent partnership video.

Katia Murmann

Journalism is at an inflection point. On one hand, globalization, digitization and social media proliferation have shortened the ‘news cycle’ to seconds; readers are looking for the latest news — in the moment it happens. On the other hand, these same forces have driven an increased appetite for highly localized, targeted information; readers are looking for stories relevant to their immediate surroundings, communities and daily lives.

As a media company, our principal challenge is to bridge this gap — to empower local newsrooms to connect high-quality, timely content to dedicated readers. While this may sound challenging enough, there’s another hurdle. An increasingly large share of advertising revenue, traditionally a principal source of funding for media companies, is being captured by Tech Giants. So, simply spending more is not an option. We must find ways of working smarter, staying agile and making informed decisions.

That’s where data comes in. Internally, we doubled-down on analytics to understand what articles and topics were driving traction, who we were reaching, where our audiences were concentrated, and so on. However, as we quickly found out, the data landscape around a website is highly complex and nuanced. We were generating more than 200 reports across a slew of dashboards and applications. The more reports we generated, the more data we had, the harder it became to manage. We did not know where to find the data we needed in this bunch of reports. But all of these reports, and all of this data, was in the hands of data analysts: not editors, not journalists. The newsroom lacked data — and we had to change this. We needed a collaborative platform that put this information directly into the hands of the content creators. That’s when we started working with Palantir.

Romain de Ganay

To get started, we sat down with journalists and editors to really understand the lifecycle of a story. Where do you find inspiration? How do you weave that idea into a story? How do you collaborate? What article features (number of images, videos, length, etc.) drive the success of an article? And, most importantly, where does the story go once you publish it? Who reads it? Why this article and not another?

Using Palantir Foundry, we were able to integrate data from Ringier’s Content Management System and in-house NLP algorithms with Blick website traffic data to create a unified ontology — a web of intuitive, human-intelligible objects such as Articles, Publishers, Journalists or Videos. We gave journalists and newsroom analysts permissioned access to this dynamic, explorable data foundation, and enabled them to build custom reports, and analytics personalized to them using this ontology. We enabled editorial teams to leverage forecasting algorithms to set their own quarterly targets, which are then written back in the ontology to be monitored.

On top of this, Palantir’s various of out-of-the-box, low code/no code workflows meant that there was no technical barrier to entry. Journalists could easily onboard to the platform and start iterating — either on their own, or with selected collaborators. This journalist-driven exploration and optimization meant we could drive enhanced performance without an algorithm dictating what reporters should write about.

Katia Murmann

Today, data is everywhere in our newsroom, in the dashboards for every journalist, in every meeting that takes place. The results are stunning. 85% of our newsroom uses Foundry on a daily basis. Since we deployed Palantir Foundry, traffic has increased — and we’ve been able to convert the additional revenue to fund independent, quality journalism. We are still at the beginning of this data driven journey. We look forward to continuing to support journalists as they produce high quality content and meet readers where they are.
