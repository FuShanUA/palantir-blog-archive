Product Reliability at Palantir: Life on the PRX team

2021-12-15 · Original →

Editor’s Note: In this blog post, Product Reliability Engineers (PREs) and Product Reliability Operations Analysts (PROs) share what it’s like to work on Palantir’s Product Reliability (PRX) team.

Palantir’s Gotham and Foundry platforms underpin mission-critical infrastructure throughout the world, whether they’re being used to deliver food assistance to remote corners of the world, increase aircraft production rates, or combat the COVID-19 pandemic. The Product Reliability (PRX) team drives stability across our products by ensuring uninterrupted customer access to Palantir platforms. Comprised of Product Reliability Engineers and Product Reliability Operations Analysts, PRX proactively and responsively assesses and addresses issues, from root causing, monitoring, and ultimately eliminating causes of instability.

What does the Product Reliability team do?

Product Reliability Engineers

Product Reliability Engineers are the first line of defense when something goes wrong. PREs are responsible for triaging, troubleshooting, and coordinating the resolution of issues. They don’t just seek one-time fixes; PREs are essential to building permanent technical solutions and often embed with specific product teams to dive deep into product issues

Product Reliability Operations Analysts

Delivering stable and reliable products to customers requires robust operational infrastructure and processes. That’s where Product Reliability Operations Analysts (PROs) come in. PROs partner with teams across the business to scale and eliminate risks rooted in internal procedures. PROs own outcomes from Day 1. Across product release management, incident response, customer facing support, and cloud operations, PROs design, operate, and optimize the processes and solutions that Palantir relies on to deliver products and support to our customers.

What does a typical day on PRX look like?

Cecelia (PRE, New York)

No two days are the same as a PRE, but the one commonality is that every day I’m focused on improving the stability of my team’s products. I spend time responding to support issues from other engineers and analyzing long-term stability trends for my team. I also contribute to bigger projects with a stability focus. Sometimes this means getting my hands dirty with technical contributions. Other times, I provide the operational support necessary to drive projects forward, creating project plans and ensuring stakeholders are aligned on the outcomes.

Jacob (PRE, London)

My job is to make sure my team is constantly improving the stability and performance of the products we manage. I look at trends in the issues we’re seeing in production, respond to support requests, and run incident retrospectives. I also make sure that the outcomes of all those actions are handled effectively. Being a PRE also means being an Incident Manager for emergencies that arise with our products, helping get those problems solved as quickly as possible.

Julia (PRE, Denver)

As the lead of the Incident Response Team, a typical day involves managing the team’s workload by meeting with stakeholders about the status of our projects and understanding what we need in order to be successful. I also spend time planning what the team will tackle over the next few months and brainstorming how to accomplish that, which is a part of my job I value because it allows me to understand the business outcomes of my team’s work. And I usually have a few 1:1 catchups with team members to see how they’re doing and how I can unblock them.

Ed (PRO, New York)

There truly isn’t a “typical day” as a PRO. You might be finding points of friction in support workflows, crafting automations, developing metrics, or planning projects to solve complex issues that internal teams are dealing with. But everything I work on relates to supporting other teams with their operational workflows, and has a positive impact on how other teams are able to be efficient in their work.

Anne (PRO, Amsterdam)

Some days I work independently, while others I collaborate with people across our Product and Business Development teams. This year, I’ve worked on our Stability team to analyze the root causes of Palantir incidents and advise our Foundry teams on the most effective investments to make to improve the stability of the platform. Some of this work is independent, like building models to analyze our outage data, but a lot of it involves collaboration with other teams. I might discuss my findings and strategies with the platform stability working group, present our advice to relevant product teams, or validate whether the investments we have made have improved user experience and platform stability.

What does growth look like in Product Reliability?

Cecelia (PRE, New York)

Product Reliability has offered me the materials I need to “level up” my technical skills at every turn, whether through mentorship from a team member on how to approach a tough problem or a week-long course on a technical subject. I’ve also grown from a strategic perspective — getting a bird’s-eye view of the stability landscape has allowed me to become more opinionated on the pressing needs of the business and drive those outcomes to completion.

Anne (PRO, Amsterdam)

Growth at Palantir is choose-your-own-adventure, and Product Reliability is no exception. On Product Reliability, we are regularly exposed to new responsibilities and new projects through our work with product teams and dedicated task forces. In my own journey, my lead helped me identify how to be more effective and maximize my impact by finding opportunities that best align with my strengths.

Jacob (PRE, London)

For me, growth has looked like gaining sharper analytical skills and becoming more confident about making high-level decisions for the teams I’m involved with. I’ve also grown in technical ability. During my time as a PRE, I’ve learned so much more about not just the database technologies my team works with but also about software development in general. By working with a number of different dev teams across both Gotham and Foundry, I’ve gained a holistic understanding of how our products work and what they actually do, which in turn has provided me with experience and understanding that I bring into each new project.

Julia (PRE, Denver)

Growth in PRX can take many different forms based on your interests and skillsets. I started my career working on our Gotham platform, then moved to one of our Business Development teams to tackle stability issues that come with managing customer environments. I grew in my responsibilities as an individual contributor by taking on mentorship and becoming an interviewer. Over time, this led me naturally toward becoming a “people lead,” or Palantir’s version of manager, and later a team lead of the Incident Response Team.

What do you like most about your role?

Jacob (PRE, London)

I enjoy the sense of ownership and agency around the work that I do. As a PRE, when I’m given a problem to solve, I’m not told how to solve that problem — most of the time, it’s a problem that hasn’t even been solved before. I have the freedom to decide how to best take the challenge on and the trust from my team that I’m doing the right thing. Granted, I don’t always do the right thing, but even then, I always know that my team has my back and that I’ll be able to learn and grow from the experience.

Cecelia (PRE, New York)

My favorite thing about PRX — and Palantir — is the people. I’m constantly surrounded by some of the smartest and most motivated people I’ve ever met, and the community is incredibly friendly and collaborative. I also like that I get to grow technically in a fast-paced environment. In addition to solving technical problems, I also get insight into many different facets of the business and feel like I get to do a little bit of everything.

Julia (PRE, Denver)

I love the variety of problems I get to work on. I truly feel like I could never get bored on PRX because there are constantly new challenges being thrown at our team. We touch every edge of the business, so I feel I could always find something new and exciting to work on. No day is ever the same at Palantir!

Anne (PRO, Amsterdam)

What I like best at Palantir is the people. People at Palantir come from a myriad of different backgrounds and everyone has their own story of how they ended up here. But what they all have in common is that they are really motivated and they really care. On PRX, you get to work with teams across the company, from product to business development to our internal teams. You get to see things from different perspectives and collaborate with people from across the business.

Ed (PRO, New York)

I came to Palantir because I wanted to help people, and the PRO role affords me the opportunity to help not just our customers but our internal teams as well. The problems I solve on a daily basis have variety, are challenging, and impact the company as a whole. I’m not stuck doing “busy work;” most of my time is spent tackling difficult issues, understanding what the root problem is, and then creating a standardized workflow or automating away the points of friction. This gives me a lot of satisfaction because I get to see the actual outcomes that I’m improving.
