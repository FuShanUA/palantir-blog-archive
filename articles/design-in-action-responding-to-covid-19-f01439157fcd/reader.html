Design in Action: Responding to COVID-19

2020-07-10 · Original →

The morning of March 27th is one that I will never forget. This was the morning that my mom was rushed to the hospital, subsequently sent to the ICU, then placed on a ventilator. Her mild cough had quickly developed into a severe fever and she was having difficulty breathing. For me, this was the moment when the COVID-19 pandemic went from a news story to a terrifyingly personal threat.

In the tumultuous days that followed, I began to reflect on how my passion and career — product design — could play a role in combatting the COVID-19 pandemic. The following days and weeks would provide an unequivocal answer to that question: product design is an incredibly powerful tool in the fight against COVID. From 3D-printed face shields to infection-tracking visualizations, the ingenuity and innovation exhibited by designers and engineers across the world has underscored the real-world applicability of the design process.

Rather than let myself be consumed by a feeling of helplessness and concern, I wanted to work together with my fellow designers in fighting this unfolding global disaster. My colleagues at Palantir have thrown themselves at the pandemic’s challenges with dedication and passion, and as a product designer, I’ve been given a unique opportunity to join in solving these pressing problems. In this post, I’ll share some capabilities we’ve configured to help critical institutions address the pandemic and talk through the role of design in making an impact on exigent problems.

On our design team, we often talk about the natural tension between local and global optimization. In practical terms, this refers to the degree to which our team is focused on one specific client’s problem versus taking a step back and identifying problems shared across multiple customers and business verticals. Identifying and solving these common problems, through the process of ideation and synthesis, feeds a healthy, long-term product strategy. While we are always focused on tackling the unique problems presented with a specific client, it’s the continuous and steady strengthening of our data platforms that provides critical improvements in functionality and capability. This approach allows us to scale our work and approach each new problem with greater speed, efficiency, and effectiveness. There’s no clearer example of this than the speed with which Palantir’s software has made an impact in the global effort to understand and respond to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Empathy is a foundational principle of product design. In the past five years that I’ve been at Palantir, I’ve found it to be a difficult yet incredibly fulfilling design challenge to put oneself in the shoes of someone with a wholly different career, background, or area of expertise. Unlike a lot of other tech companies in Silicon Valley, Palantir’s users are not always tech-savvy consumers; for example, they are aerospace engineers who devote years to developing the perfect airflow around a wing. The users of our software can name each of the key individuals in an international human trafficking network. The users of our software put their lives on the line every day to defend our national security. I respect and admire their work, but it often feels distant from my own life. Now, I found myself identifying deeply with our users: procurement specialists seeking N95 masks for physicians, epidemiologists looking for trends in the spread of the disease, and others. As I watched them run toward the proverbial burning house, I felt immensely grateful that my colleagues and I could support them.

Multiple countries’ national health organizations are using our platform, Palantir Foundry, to securely integrate their critical data sources for disease tracking, Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) supply chain, and hospital capacities. By using Foundry to integrate their data sources, our clients create a data backbone powering a suite of new operational applications, including the Allocation App, which our team was also able to design and configure in hours using a new app building tool called Foundry Workshop.

The Allocation App utilizes health organizations’ real-time PPE supply data on national stockpiles and manufacturer capacity to explore allocation strategies across states or counties. Users can input various allocation criteria, such as case growth rate or total case count, to determine how much of each supply type could be sent to various affected regions. Allocation decisions themselves are also captured in the platform for later analysis to determine whether the selected strategy had the intended effect, or if it should be altered going forward. From a design perspective, we worked together with the actual users responsible for allocating PPE to craft a user experience that presents the critical information in an intuitive and efficient way.

This critical product design lift can also be seen in our recent work with U.S. Northern Command (USNORTHCOM) in their response to the outbreak in New York City. Having lived in NYC for five years, this also hit incredibly close to home. As the crisis in the city deepened, emergency medical personnel and logistics experts from across the US Department of Defense (DoD) were called upon to help local authorities in response to the unfolding humanitarian disaster. Coordinating such a massive effort, which included deploying hospital ships and converting a convention center into a field hospital, was not only a communication challenge but also a huge logistics and data analysis problem.

Fortunately, for many years, our design team has been working to build capabilities for real-time operational planning and analysis in our Gotham platform. We have spent hundreds of hours researching and ideating on the user interface of these applications in an effort to make them as walk-up usable as possible. In days, we were able to deploy our software to provide USNORTHCOM’s senior decision-makers and users on the ground with a comprehensive operating picture of hotspots and real-time personnel status. Critically, our platform’s granular access controls ensure that users see only the data they are authorized to see. In the midst of a crisis, the intuitiveness of a platform is critical. Every hour that users saved in needing to learn a new tool could be spent providing critical relief.

All of this rapid product development could not have happened without inspired product design. Real world impact on this scale and speed is only possible thanks to the culmination of years of talented designers laying foundations such as data pipeline tools, security principles, and analytical applications.

Fortunately, after spending days on a ventilator, my mom is recovering from this horrible disease. In the immediate aftermath of this personal experience, I am incredibly proud to be a part of a team that’s doing everything in our ability to prevent others from having to go through this. This is only possible by partnering with some of the most important and impactful organizations in the world, which is a privilege that our team does not take lightly.
