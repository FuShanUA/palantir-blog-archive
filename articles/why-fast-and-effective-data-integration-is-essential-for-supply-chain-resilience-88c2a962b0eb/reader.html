Why Fast and Effective Data Integration is Essential for Supply Chain Resilience

2021-12-10 · Original →

Covid-19 has proven the immense impact that disrupted supply chains can have on all aspects of life, including the delivery of critical services

Over the past few months, society has viscerally and abruptly come to understand that robust, functioning supply chains are essential to our modern way of life.

An ongoing and urgent challenge that supply chains face is that they often lack structural resilience, which is essential in the current environment of disruption.

Through our work with several multinational organisations, we’ve seen a pattern indicative of a brittle supply chain: chiefly, a fractured and disconnected data landscape. We often find that procurement, purchasing, transportation and logistics, warehouse management, and production teams — along with many other stakeholders — are storing their data in siloed systems. A single organisation will typically have multiple enterprise resource planning systems, transport and warehouse management systems, Microsoft Excel files on individuals’ desktops, plus many other types of data spread across departments. This leads to making siloed decisions, without vital context.

And that’s just the tip of the iceberg: the sheer interconnectedness and interdependence of modern supply chains goes way beyond the capacity of inflexible software written back in the 1990s. The more complex our supply chains grow, the more risks they generate, and the greater the chance of organisation-critical disruption. The old way of managing the supply chain no longer works, and the implications of getting it wrong can have severe, life-changing impacts.

Palantir Foundry addresses this fundamental challenge by building a digital twin of the entire supply chain — from the hand of the supplier to the hand of the customer. The digital twin is then directed at solving discrete supply chain challenges by connecting analytics and operations. This is exactly what we did with NHS England to secure the rapid, accurate, and effective delivery of the NHS’s largest-ever vaccination programme.

Suddenly, the NHS’s mandate widened from patient care to an incredibly complex new mission of delivering tens of millions of vaccine doses against an aggressive timeframe.

The NHS didn’t have the luxury of working within a greenfield project, instead having to incorporate hundreds of existing data sources in a matter of days. The work was taking place in an ever-changing landscape of urgency and uncertainty, with levels of vaccine supply, storage requirements, and availability of key resources like PPE in a constant state of flux.

Using Foundry, the NHS integrated, cleaned and harmonised its disparate data sources across the supply chain to create a single, reliable set of information to inform decision-making. From there, they could track every aspect of the vaccination programme, including the number of vaccines being administered and the volume of vaccinations by site, geography and delivery model. This allowed the NHS to adjust their approach in real time and identify areas where more support was needed. The digital twin of the end-to-end supply chain allowed front-line services to deliver vaccines at record speed across the country.
