In Pursuit of Information Dominance

2021-07-30 · Original →

Palantir helps NORAD and USNORTHCOM deter, detect, and defeat threats to the United States

Snapshots from the Global Information Dominance Experiment (GIDE) series

The best part about working at Palantir is working side-by-side with customers to solve their hardest problems. Not only do we deploy amazing commercial software, but we embed closely with our customers as they continue to tackle complex problem sets and we help identify solutions. Through these close partnerships, where collaboration is key, we iterate to solve problems at the edge, while also moving core product platform capabilities forward at almost unheard-of speeds. Perhaps there’s no better example of this tight iteration loop than our current work with North American Aerospace Defense Command (NORAD) and United States Northern Command (USNORTHCOM), the Nation’s military defense commands for North America.

Crisis simulations test military decision making

USNORTHCOM’s mission is to deter, detect, and defeat threats to the United States. As military leaders address the rising threat of peer adversaries and the evolution from small wars and counterterrorism to nation state deterrence, reliable technology that centralizes “truth” and powers fast decision making is more important than ever. To showcase these decision making capabilities, NORTHCOM recently hosted a series of Global Information Dominance Experiments (GIDE) with all major Combatant Commands, allied partners, government programs of record, and various industry partners.

Leveraging the power of Palantir’s application programming interfaces (APIs) and our open data standard architecture, leaders are able to conduct simulated decision making exercises in real time that mimic real world scenarios and also provide the ability to train AI/ML algorithms through repeatable scenario workflows.

Palantir powers insights across military commands

Not only is our technology empowering elements of USNORTHCOM, it’s also providing a key ability to plan, collaborate, and execute missions across multiple Combatant Commands.

Beginning in December 2020, Palantirians from across our U.S. Government team came together with NORAD and USNORTHCOM to enable permissioned data sharing, collaboration, and globally integrated decision making within the DoD via a series of these GIDE exercises. GIDE 1 took place with four Combatant Commands, and GIDE 2 expanded to all 11 Combatant Commands. During the GIDE 3 exercise, Combatant Commands worked across scenarios that addressed contested logistics, staffing and deployment of resources, and AI-enabled decision making. As these experiments evolve, Palantir continues to iterate with our partners to ensure military decision makers are equipped to make the fastest and most informed decisions. Through this partnership, Palantir engineers helped deploy software capabilities to help USNORTHCOM, its partner Combatant Commands, and allied nations in demonstrating three key goals: Domain Awareness (what we know), Information Dominance (faster, better knowledge than our adversary), and Decision Superiority (the quickest decision execution).

Timely information drives coordinated action to thwart crisis

Domain Awareness. By providing collaborative workspaces through our data platform, Palantir enabled 11 Combatant Commands to conduct real time planning and mission execution with a full understanding of the battlefield. So why is our work here important? Many of the military staff sections plan across a variety of tools specific to their needs, all manifesting in larger decision briefings that collate data, sometimes by hand, on personnel, intelligence, operations, logistics, etc. The problem with this process is that commanders can make critical decisions drawing on stale information. For instance, if a planning cycle takes 12 hours to culminate in a decision brief to the commander, his or her decision is at least 12 hours stale. Instead, Palantir integrates data from across Commands, tools and formats into a single pane of glass, enabling staff sections and decision makers to work collaboratively with real time information. Put simply, Palantir’s contributions to domain awareness helps leaders to make split second decisions — with high confidence in their data — when those decisions could mean the difference between war and peace.

Information Dominance. Palantir helps commanders shorten the time it takes to plan and make decisions by leveraging sound data integration and automating parts of the process with artificial intelligence and machine learning (AI/ML). Time saved can be spent making more decisions, at a faster rate. Additionally, Palantir allows for global strategic planning so that commanders can be proactive, not reactive. As warfare evolves away from counterinsurgency to nation state deterrence, faster decision ability empowers leaders to deter in competition, de-escalate in crisis, and defeat in conflict.

Decision Superiority. In each of the GIDE experiments, Palantir worked with our government partners to support progress on Joint All-Domain Command and Control (JADC2) objectives. While some of these objectives vary in interpretation and execution, Palantir provided the data foundation that converges Intelligence Surveillance, Reconnaissance (ISR), Command and Control (C2), operational readiness data, and DoD investments in AI and ML. With this fusion of data, commanders are able to develop multiple courses of actions to inform fast and effective decisions with full understanding and trust in their data.

Why it matters

One of the things we love most about working at Palantir is our ability to impact real world outcomes at a moment’s notice. When we started this journey with NORTHCOM in December, the E (experiment) in GIDE was front of mind. Through close partnership, hard work, and iteration with the Combatant Commands we’re now able to use our APIs and open data architecture to aid decision advantage without limit. As dependency on AI/ML in military decision making grows, Palantir remains committed to creating, testing, and refining repeatable data workflows to help commanders win. While GIDE tested our capabilities through simulated environments, it’s exciting to know our capabilities are being used right now to keep our country safe.
