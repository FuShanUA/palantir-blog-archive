For Green and Equitable Roadways: Introducing the EV Infrastructure OS

2022-01-11 · Original →

The electrification of America with Electric Vehicle (EV) charging stations could be the greatest investment in mobility infrastructure in the country since the construction of the Interstate Highway System in the mid 20th century. Through their construction and beyond, interstate highways expanded the potential of the personal vehicle, making it a ubiquitous part of American life.

Today’s emerging grid of charging stations promises to do the same for the EV. With robust and far-reaching charging networks, EVs could one day inherit and push forward the legacy of American car culture — the long road trips that enable commerce and bring people together, and the horizon of opportunity provided by the freedom to go anywhere, cheaply and safely, at any time.

Half a century later, the flaws and shortcomings of the Interstate Highway system have been extensively studied. The standardized federal project often collided with local conditions, erasing entire urban neighborhoods while leading to the decline of many other communities underserved by the road network. Charging networks and EVs offer the promise of clean and cheap transportation, but as massive infrastructure projects, they present both old and new problems that require innovative solutions.

To empower businesses and organizations to take on these challenges, Palantir has unveiled the EV Infrastructure Operating System (OS), an application that can help ensure that future investments in EV infrastructure are efficient, equitable, and sustainable.

Unlike the federal project of the Interstate Highway System, the electrification of American roads requires a new platform to enable decentralized, public-private collaboration involving many different stakeholders — from energy utilities and city authorities to network operators, retailers, and more. Charging networks also need to be built in response to local conditions, and to provide equitable access to the benefits of electric mobility. With its ability to layer multiple internal and external sources of data under a single operating picture, the EV Infrastructure OS can make this vision a reality.

The OS integrates internal, open source, and 3rd party data through Palantir Foundry for operational decision-making, with the option to add Wejo’s robust connected vehicle data — including billions of near-real-time data points. Together, Palantir’s software and Wejo’s data enable companies to expand into new geographies and manage infrastructure through an integrated platform for optimal decision-making. With advanced site selection and network operations tools, any business or organization can use the OS to build, scale, and operate first-class EV charging infrastructure.
